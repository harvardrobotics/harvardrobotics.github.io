Personal Compliance Notice · Directive 2026-HR-001

You Are Required to Acquire One (1) Humanoid Robotic Assistant

Effective January 1, 2027 · Harvard University Policy on Research Modernization

Dear Harvard Researcher,

Following the success of humanoid robots in surgical suites and at peer institutions, Harvard's Provost Office has determined that every active researcher must be personally assigned one (1) certified Humanoid Robotic Research Assistant 🤖, hereinafter "your robot."

An internal audit found that the average Harvard researcher wastes 14 hours per week on tasks beneath their intellectual capacity, including fetching papers, refilling coffee, and pretending to laugh at their PI's jokes during lab meetings. Your robot will handle all of this. It will not laugh at the jokes either, but it will nod at statistically appropriate intervals.

"The modern Harvard researcher should not be fetching their own oat milk. A robot can do this better, while simultaneously reviewing your literature and maintaining an appropriately enthusiastic expression during Zoom calls."

Harvard 2025 Research Efficiency Task Force, Final Report, p. 47

Your robot will personally:

  • Attend meetings on your behalf when you are "feeling unwell" (i.e., a deadline exists)
  • Write your Specific Aims. It will do this better than you. Please sit with that.
  • Manage your Slack, reply to "quick question" emails, and discourage reviewers from requesting more experiments
  • Receive a window desk. It doesn't need sunlight. It simply deserves it.
